Title: The bottle flip was not what I expected to remember
Link: <https://lnkd.in/p/gHExv4RB>
--------------------------------

Out of everything that happened at the Kerala Traders Carnival yesterday, the bottle flip is somehow the first thing that comes to mind.

Shravan walked past, saw us playing, joined in, landed one, celebrated with the rest of us, and walked away like nothing had happened.

About 30 minutes later... the same guy was on stage.

Safe to say, I wasn't expecting that.

One thing from his talk stayed with me.

He spoke about how his journey started with something as simple as naming his venture and creating an Instagram page.

Seeing everything he's already built at 18 made that hit a little differently.

Somewhere in between, a few of us volunteers looked at each other and laughed,

"What were we even doing at 18 or 19?" 😂

Then there was Hilal.

During one of the panel discussions, he stood up to ask a question.

That question somehow turned into a conversation. The conversation turned into curiosity. And before long, he had 10 minutes on stage to present what he was building.

The product was almost secondary.

What stayed with me was watching someone create an opportunity instead of waiting for one.

You could tell he was searching for words at times. It wasn't a perfectly polished pitch.

He just stood there and delivered.

Ironically, I didn't really learn much about trading yesterday.

I was behind the camera for most of the event.

But I came back thinking about something completely different.

I think I'd always imagined people who build things as people who knew exactly what they were doing before they started.

Yesterday didn't completely change that.

It just made that feel... a little less true. :)
